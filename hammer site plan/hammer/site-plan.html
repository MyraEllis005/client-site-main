Content

Home page

[Purpose: This website is created for those who want to learn or improve snowboard skills. No matter what skill level you are at, this website will be able to cater to your needs by teaching fundamental skills necessary to complete that next trick! How this website works: There are two other pages in this website, the first one, “Beginner”, teaches people who have typically been snowboarding for 1 year or less. The next page “Intermediate” teaches specific tricks to help you fine tune your snowboarding. Within each page, there are a series of levels that describe a rider's capabilities. This will allow you to pinpoint your ability and teach you what tricks you should be working on. How do I know what level I am? Beginner snowboarders are considered those who have just started snowboarding all the way to those who have just learned how to carve and can catch air. If you are comfortable with catching air and you can confidently ride toe-edge, then you can focus on progressing your riding! ]

Images for the Home page

[Page 2]

[Beginner snowboarding focuses on those who have just started riding and want to improve their fundamental skills. The Levels of this page consist of 1, 2 and 3. A level 1 rider has just started snowboarding and needs to learn the fundamentals. Before moving to level 2, a level 1 rider must be comfortable on their heel edge. A level 2 rider can confidently do the falling leaf snowboard style on their heel edge and can stay standing when they are stopped. They should be starting to ride on their toe edge. A level 3 rider can ride the falling leaf completely on their toe-edge and maintain control the entire time. They can link turns and carve confidently. They are able to do any of the designated runs at Kelly Canyon confidently. They have started to catch air on jumps. Lessons Level 1 Falling leaf Heelside J turn Heelside Snowplow Level 2 Falling Leaf Toe edge J turn Toe Side Reverse Snowplow Linking Turns Level 3 Catch air on small features Carve down difficult terrain ]

Images for the Page 2

[Page 3]

[Intermediate snowboarders should have all of the basic snowboard skills mastered. An intermediate rider should be comfortable straight airing jumps and making all types of turns. This page describes the different levels of intermediate snowboarding. A level 1 Intermediate snowboarder is confident straight airing small jumps. They can come in to jumps confidently on their toe or heel edge. These intermediate riders have started to ride switch and practice 180s. A level 2 intermediate snowboarder can confidently do regular and switch frontside 180s. They are comfortable hitting boxes and low consequence tubes. They are learning backside 180s and 360s. A level 3 intermediate rider is capable of doing 360s and backside 180s off of smaller jumps. They can ride entire runs switch without falling. They have started trying backflips and tame dogs and have landed at least one of them. Advanced riders include those who are able to land inverted flips confidently. They have started throwing off-axis variations of spins such as frontside and backside rodeos or frontside and backside corks. Intermediate Level 1 Frontside 180s Cab 180s Hit boxes Hit tubes Level 2 Frontside or Backside 360s Backflip or Tamedog Sitch and Regular backside 180s Level 3 Hand rail 50-50s Backside Tricks Inverted Flips ]
