Intermediate snowboarders should have all of the basic snowboard skills mastered. An intermediate rider should be comfortable straight airing jumps and making all types of turns. This page describes the different levels of intermediate snowboarding.

Level 1

A level 1 Intermediate snowboarder is confident straight airing small jumps. They can come into jumps confidently on their toe or heel edge. These intermediate riders have started to ride switch and practice 180s.

Level 1: Frontside 180s, Cab 180s, Hit boxes, Hit tubes

Level 2

A level 2 intermediate snowboarder can confidently do regular and switch frontside 180s. They are comfortable hitting boxes and low-consequence tubes. They are learning backside 180s and 360s.

Level 2: Frontside or Backside 360s, Backflip or Tamedog, Switch and Regular backside 180s

Level 3

A level 3 intermediate rider is capable of doing 360s and backside 180s off of smaller jumps. They can ride entire runs switch without falling. They have started trying backflips and tame dogs and have landed at least one of them. Advanced riders include those who are able to land inverted flips confidently. They have started throwing off-axis variations of spins such as frontside and backside rodeos or frontside and backside corks.

Level 3: Hand rail 50-50s, Backside Tricks, Inverted Flips
